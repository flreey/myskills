# Image / Visual Template

## Vague input
> "Make a depressing image."

## Questions to ask (≤3)
1. What's the depressing thing about? (loneliness / loss / failure / collapse / "choose for me")
2. Realistic photo, painting, or illustration? ("choose for me" → editorial photo)
3. Where will it be used? (social post / poster / book cover / mood board)

## Final prompt (model-portable)

```text
Goal:
A single still image that conveys the specific feeling of "quiet failure" — not generic sadness.

Concept sentence:
A man sits at a kitchen table at 2 AM, staring at a rejected job application on his laptop. The room is otherwise dark.

Subject relationship:
One human, isolated. No other figures. The laptop screen is the only light source.

Moment:
The pause right after reading the rejection — before any reaction. Hands still on the keyboard, eyes unfocused.

Visual metaphor:
The single warm screen-glow against a cold blue room = hope eaten by reality.

Composition:
Wide shot, subject off-center (rule of thirds, left), negative space dominates the right two-thirds. Camera at table height.

Color:
Cool blues and grays (room) + one warm amber (screen). No saturated colors elsewhere.

Style:
Editorial photography, 35mm, shallow depth of field, grain visible. Reference: Gregory Crewdson's domestic interiors.

Avoid (negative prompt):
- No tears, no dramatic poses, no clichés (broken mirrors, rain on window, etc.)
- No text, no logos
- No bright/cheerful color accidentally creeping in
- No multiple people, no pets

Acceptance criteria:
- Viewer feels the situation in <2 seconds without needing caption
- The screen glow is the brightest thing in frame
- The mood reads as "exhausted" not "tragic"
```

## Notes
- Concept sentence > style words. "Quiet failure at 2 AM" beats "depressing, dark, moody."
- Always include a negative prompt — it's where most generic outputs leak in.
- One sharp metaphor beats three vague ones.
